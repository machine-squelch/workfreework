## Launch Week

### Day 1 - Soft Launch
- [ ] Send email to personal network (50-100 people)
- [ ] Post on personal social media
- [ ] Share in relevant Slack/Discord communities
- [ ] Message: "Hey, I built this. Would love your feedback."

### Day 2-3 - Community Seeding
- [ ] Post launch essay on:
  - [ ] Hacker News
  - [ ] Reddit (r/Entrepreneur, r/SideProject, r/Automation)
  - [ ] IndieHackers
- [ ] Engage with comments and feedback
- [ ] Fix any bugs reported

### Day 4-5 - Content Push
- [ ] Publish second essay
- [ ] Share on LinkedIn with professional angle
- [ ] Cross-post to Medium (with canonical link)
- [ ] Email early subscribers

### Day 6-7 - Paid Testing
- [ ] Launch small ad campaign ($50-200)
  - [ ] Facebook/Instagram: target automation enthusiasts
  - [ ] Twitter: promote best-performing post
- [ ] Track conversion rates
- [ ] Optimize based on data

## Things NOT to Do at Launch

❌ Don't wait for perfection - ship with 3 essays, not 30
❌ Don't buy followers or fake engagement
❌ Don't spam communities with low-quality posts
❌ Don't launch on all platforms at once - focus on 2-3
❌ Don't ignore feedback - respond to everyone
❌ Don't get discouraged by low initial traffic - it takes time

---

## Backup Plan (If Launch Flops)

If first week gets <100 visitors:

1. **Double down on content quality**
   - Publish your best essay yet
   - Share more personal stories
   - Add more data and research

2. **Engage more actively**
   - Comment on related Reddit posts
   - Reply to Twitter threads
   - Join relevant Discord/Slack groups

3. **Pivot messaging**
   - Test different headlines
   - Try different social angles
   - A/B test email capture copy

4. **Reach out directly**
   - Email 50 people personally
   - Ask for specific feedback
   - Offer to help them automate something

## Post-Launch Iteration

After first week, review:
- Which content performed best?
- Where did traffic come from?
- What feedback did you get?
- What needs immediate fixing?

Then:
- Double down on what worked
- Fix critical issues
- Publish next piece of content
- Keep shipping weekly

---

**Remember:** Launch is just the beginning. Consistent, quality content over 6-12 months is what builds an audience.

Stay focused. Keep shipping. Respond to everyone.
