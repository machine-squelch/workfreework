# Content Creation Guide

A guide for creating and managing content for WorkFreeWork.

## Content Pillars

All content should fall into one of these categories:

1. **Automation Case Studies** - Real companies/roles being automated
2. **Replacement Playbooks** - What to learn, how to re-skill, side-income ideas
3. **Policy & Ethics** - UBI experiments, labor law changes, future of work policy
4. **Tools & How-Tos** - Templates, prompt libraries, automation audits
5. **Interviews** - People who left jobs, builders, union folks, policy nerds

## Voice & Tone

### ✅ Do This
- Sharp, direct, honest
- Equal parts analysis and sarcasm
- Practical and actionable
- Critical but not cynical
- Empowering, not preachy

### ❌ Not This
- MBA jargon ("synergy", "thought leadership")
- Hustle culture ("rise and grind", "10x")
- Empty motivation ("you can do anything!")
- Crypto-bro energy
- Academic gatekeeping

## Content Templates

### Essay Structure

```markdown
# [Title: Clear, Provocative, Specific]

[Hook: One strong opening paragraph]

## The Problem

[What's broken? What needs addressing?]

## Why This Matters

[Personal stakes, broader implications]

## What Most People Get Wrong

[Challenge common assumptions]

## The Data / Evidence

[Facts, case studies, numbers]

## What To Do About It

[Actionable steps, specific tactics]

## The Opportunity

[Forward-looking optimism, concrete vision]
```

### Case Study Structure

```markdown
# How [Person/Company] Automated [X]% of [Task]

**Context:** Role, industry, team size

**Before:** Time spent, pain points, manual processes

**Solution:** Tools used, automation setup, implementation time

**Results:** Hours saved, money saved, quality improvements

**Lessons Learned:** What worked, what didn't, advice for others

**Tools Used:** [List with links]

**Resources:** [Templates, scripts, configs]
```

### Tool Review Structure

```markdown
# [Tool Name]: [One-Line Value Prop]

**What it does:** [Clear explanation]

**Best for:** [Specific use cases]

**Pricing:** [Transparent breakdown]

**Pros:**
- [Specific benefits]

**Cons:**
- [Honest limitations]

**Real-World Use:**
[Personal experience or case study]

**Verdict:** [Recommend or skip, with context]
```

## Writing Process

### 1. Research Phase (30-60 min)
- Collect data, examples, case studies
- Interview subjects (if applicable)
- Test tools (for reviews)
- Gather screenshots/assets

### 2. Outline Phase (15 min)
- Create clear section headers
- Note key points per section
- Identify data/quotes to include

### 3. First Draft (60-90 min)
- Write without editing
- Get thoughts out quickly
- Focus on clarity over cleverness

### 4. Edit Phase (30-45 min)
- Cut fluff and jargon
- Strengthen opening/closing
- Add specific examples
- Check for voice consistency

### 5. SEO & Format (15 min)
- Add meta description
- Optimize title for search
- Add internal links
- Format for readability

## Headline Formulas

### ✅ Strong Headlines
- "[X] Didn't Take Your Job — [Y] Did"
- "How to [Benefit] Without [Common Pain Point]"
- "[Number] [Tools/Tactics] That Actually [Result]"
- "Why [Common Belief] Is Wrong About [Topic]"
- "The [Adjective] Guide to [Outcome]"

### ❌ Weak Headlines
- "Thoughts on Automation"
- "My Journey to Freedom"
- "You Need to Know This"
- "The Future Is Here"

## Content Checklist

Before publishing, verify:

- [ ] Headline is clear and specific
- [ ] Opening hooks reader in first paragraph
- [ ] Includes actionable takeaways
- [ ] Data/examples support claims
- [ ] Voice is consistent with brand
- [ ] No jargon or buzzwords
- [ ] Proper formatting (headers, lists, emphasis)
- [ ] Internal links to other content
- [ ] CTA at end (newsletter signup)
- [ ] Meta description written
- [ ] Images/screenshots added
- [ ] Proofread for typos

## Guest Post Opportunities

Topics that work well as guest posts:
- UBI and policy analysis → Policy blogs
- Automation tools → Tech blogs
- Side income tactics → Personal finance blogs
- Future of work → Business publications

## Reusing Content

Maximize each piece of content:

1. **Essay** → Newsletter → Twitter thread → LinkedIn post
2. **Case study** → Tool review → Email sequence
3. **Interview** → Quote graphics → Newsletter feature
4. **Playbook** → Email course → YouTube series

## Quality Standards

### Minimum Requirements
- 1,000+ words for essays
- 500+ words for case studies
- 300+ words for tool reviews
- At least one external source cited
- At least one actionable takeaway

### Excellence Indicators
- Original data or research
- Exclusive interviews
- Custom graphics/screenshots
- Step-by-step instructions
- Downloadable resources

---

**Remember:** Every piece of content should help someone automate work, build alternative income, or understand the post-work economy better.

If it doesn't do at least one of those things, don't publish it.
